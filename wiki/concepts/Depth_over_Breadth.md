# Depth over Breadth

## Definition

The strategic principle that consumer brands in India should concentrate on a small number of high-performing SKUs rather than expanding into many categories. Niche depth at 5M customers can build ₹500 Cr+ businesses profitably — it's no longer a penetration game requiring 100M customers.

## The "Power 7" Strategy (Pilgrim Example)

After hitting ₹100 Cr with lots of hustle and experimentation, Pilgrim founder Anurag narrowed to a "Power 7" SKU strategy:
- 1 supernova SKU at ₹300 Cr
- 2-3 SKUs at ₹150-200 Cr
- 2-3 SKUs at ₹100 Cr
- 1 SKU at ₹50 Cr growing 20% MoM

Result: 1,000+ Cr business from just 7 SKUs. Strict discipline — refuses to expand to 15 — [[Source: "The Indian Consumer at 2030" Report by Fireside Ventures]].

## Why Depth Wins

- **[[Minimalist]]** built ₹500 Cr with just 25 SKUs — reason Unilever acquired it (depth, not breadth)
- **Under Need (Anderneed):** Only shapewear. Refused to expand to innerwear/loungewear. Deep in niche.
- **Moxie:** Just hair products. Deep in one category.
- **Formula:** 5M customers × 4 purchases/year × $10/purchase = $200M business

## False Adjacency Trap

The most common founder mistake is expanding to adjacent categories that share the same customer but not the same need state:
- **Bombay Shaving Company:** Razors → beard oil/face wash was a FALSE adjacency. Correct adjacency was razors → hair removal (trimmers)
- **General rule:** Manufacturer-first adjacency (because factory can make it) always fails. Consumer-first adjacency (consumer journey fit) wins
- Hair serum → shilajit is NOT valid even if customer overlaps

— [[Source: "The Indian Consumer at 2030" Report by Fireside Ventures]]

## Quality of Revenue Framework

[[Fireside_Ventures|Fireside Ventures]] evaluates early brands (₹40-50L monthly revenue) on:
1. **Channel concentration:** ₹50L from D2C + one channel > ₹50L spread across 5 channels at ₹5L each
2. **SKU concentration:** 2-3 SKUs driving ₹30-35L of ₹50L total = strong PMF signal

— [[Source: "The Indian Consumer at 2030" Report by Fireside Ventures]]
