# Credit-Driven Consumption

## Definition

The structural shift in Indian consumer behavior from savings-first to credit-first spending, mirroring US consumer patterns. India's household savings rate has fallen to historic lows while credit-based transactions (credit cards, EMI, BNPL, credit on UPI) are growing faster than debit/UPI-based spending.

## Key Data Points (2025 Festive Season)

- India's savings rate down to ~88.5%, lowest on record
- Credit card + e-commerce transactions up 27% YoY by volume during festive season
- UPI growth slowed from 50% YoY to 20% YoY, while credit card growth is accelerating — indicating consumers shifting from debit to credit instruments
- Credit on UPI rollout expected to accelerate this further
- All data from [[Source: Deep Dive into India's Consumption Story This Festive Season]]

## Mechanism

Card-linked offers during festive sales (Flipkart card offers, bank tie-ups) create a "primary card switching" effect — consumers move more spending to whichever card offers festive discounts, increasing credit penetration. BNPL and EMI options on low-value transactions (₹2,000-3,000) are also penetrating deeper.

## Implications

The hosts predict India's debt-to-GDP ratio will eventually resemble the US if current trends continue. India is mimicking US consumer behavior more than China — no super-apps working, but many individual brands thriving, and brand.com shopping is gaining traction (unlike in China where it failed) — [[Source: Deep Dive into India's Consumption Story This Festive Season]].
