# Helmer's 7 Powers

## Definition

Hamilton Helmer's framework of seven sources of durable competitive advantage (moats): Scale Economics, Network Economics, Counter-Positioning, Switching Costs, Branding, Cornered Resource, and Process Power. Applied by the ASYMMETRIC hosts to evaluate Indian companies.

## The Seven Powers

| Power | Definition | Indian Example |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| Scale Economics | Unit cost declines as volume grows | [[Lenskart]] — new factory every 2 years, declining unit cost |
| Network Economics | Product improves as more users join | [[GoKwik]] — 27 features, each new client benefits all |
| Counter-Positioning | Incumbent can't adopt your model without cannibalizing | Every new D2C brand starts here (e.g., Moxie targeting only curly hair) |
| Switching Costs | Costly for customer to leave | Zerodha — demat transfer is a 30-day manual process |
| Branding | Customer willing to pay premium for the name | Ray-Ban (yes), Lenskart (no — it's a "retail destination," not a product brand) |
| Cornered Resource | Exclusive access to a key resource | — |
| Process Power | Institutional routines that deliver superior outcomes | [[Mamaearth]] — 9 days working capital for complex SKU portfolio = "insane" |

## Key Insight: Moats Are NOT Permanent

The biggest gap in how people apply the framework. For non-winner-take-all businesses (consumer brands), moats change over time:
- Counter-positioning erodes as 20+ competitors copy your niche with ₹2-4 Cr of capital and Meta ads
- Scale economics might not matter at ₹100 Cr but become a moat at ₹5,000 Cr
- Capital allocation (AOP) should be directed toward moat-strengthening vectors, not just revenue growth

## IndiGo: India's Best Moat Case Study

Scores ~8/10 on ALL seven powers:
- **Scale:** Two-thirds market share in a duopoly
- **Network:** Tier-2 routing advantages
- **Counter-positioning:** Started with ₹100 Cr of "street funding," brutally finished competition (Kingfisher, Jet, GoAir, SpiceJet all dead)
- **Brand:** Achieved in aviation (extremely hard) — even on sandwich wrappers
- **Process power:** 3-day operational crisis resolved completely; returned to normal within days

## AOP as Moat-Building Tool

The What/How/When framework for Annual Operating Plans:
- Becomes meaningful only after ~₹100-120 Cr revenue
- "What" (direction/targets): should have 100% accuracy
- "How" (execution): the input that needs clarity before outputs
- AOP is fundamentally a capital allocation problem — allocate toward moat-strengthening, not just growth
