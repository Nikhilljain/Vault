# India Healthcare Supply Gap

## Definition

India's healthcare system is fundamentally supply-constrained: the country needs ~4 million hospital beds but has only ~1 million, creating a deficit of 3 million beds. This means even mediocre hospitals always have patients, removing competitive pressure to innovate.

## Scale of the Problem

- Total hospital industry: ~$100B; corporate hospitals only ~10%
- Largest chain (Apollo/Manipal): only ~10,000 beds each
- India needs ~$700B in capex to fill the bed deficit
- PE has invested ~$10.5B but mostly in acquisitions — net new beds to India barely growing
- Only 125,000 medical seats; takes 15 years to become a specialist doctor

## Why Hospital Capex is Bloated

Traditional hospital build: ~66% of capex goes to land + construction, which has zero bearing on care quality. Real example:
- Land opposite Magnolias, Gurgaon: ₹800 Cr
- 3 years litigation
- Construction: ₹250 Cr
- Equipment: ₹250 Cr
- Total: ₹1,700-1,800 Cr for 800-900 beds = ~₹2 Cr/bed

## The Alternative Model (Super Health)

- Cost per bed: 65-70% lower than corporate hospitals
- Built entire hospital in 4 months using long-term leases
- ~$1M per unit outflow (vs hundreds of crores for traditional)
- 3.5-4 people per bed (vs 7 at Apollo, excluding doctors)
- Eliminated: registration, billing, insurance, printing departments
- Fixed pricing: one price per surgery regardless of complications

All data from [[Source: Inside India's Healthcare Stack — Capex-Heavy Hospitals, Supply Gaps & Venture-Scale Hospital Alternatives]].

## Insurance Economics Are Broken

- IRDAI claim ratios: 90-110% (premium collected vs payout)
- Insurance needs ~50% margin to be viable; best hospital (Max) does only 30% EBITDA
- Driving full-stack integration: PB Fintech investing $200M+ in hospitals; Narayana Health launching insurance
- The problem isn't lack of insurance — it's that healthcare delivery costs are inflated

## Friction, Not Cost, Kills Compliance

- Diabetes medication adherence drops after 92 days on average (lifelong medication)
- Auto-shipping experiment: 80% compliance after 6 months (vs near-zero without)
- Even billionaires face same wait-time friction
- Fear of diagnosis is the bigger barrier — Apollo had to threaten salary withholding for free employee checkups, got only 60% compliance
