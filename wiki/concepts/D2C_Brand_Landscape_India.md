# D2C Brand Landscape India

## Definition

The ecosystem of direct-to-consumer brands in India — companies founded in the last ~10 years that sell primarily through online channels (own website + marketplaces) rather than traditional retail distribution. As of 2025, this is a ~$15-20B total revenue segment (all sales, not just D2C channel).

## Scale and Growth

- ~160 new-age D2C brands collectively generate ₹42,000 Cr annually, growing 15-20%
- Legacy FMCG companies (ex-top 3) generate ~₹45,000 Cr, growing at only ~5%
- The long tail of new-age brands is now comparable in size to legacy companies
- All online-first, whereas legacy companies are offline-heavy (shifting to quickcom and ecom but slowly)
- [[Source: Deep Dive into India's Consumption Story This Festive Season]]

## D2C vs Marketplace Debate

A persistent tension in Indian D2C: no brand is more than 20-25% D2C (own website) in revenue. The rest flows through marketplaces (Amazon, Flipkart) and increasingly quick commerce (Blinkit, Zepto). The hosts argue:

- **China model:** Brand.com never took off because T-mall functions as a mall (brand stores within platform), removing the need for own websites
- **US model:** Brand.com thrives — companies like AG1 and Hims are approaching $1B revenue, D2C-only, profitable
- **India model:** Trending toward US, but retail channels (quick commerce, marketplaces) are getting so strong that not being on Blinkit/Amazon means losing market share. Brands are forced to solve for marketplace presence, which pulls investment away from D2C — [[Source: Deep Dive into India's Consumption Story This Festive Season]]

**US benchmarks exist:** AG1 and Hims are both approaching ~$1B revenue, D2C-only (brand.com), and profitable. No Indian equivalent yet — but the hosts believe this will happen in India within 10 years as market depth increases and consumers become willing to pay for high-efficacy products (skincare, protein, etc.) on brand.com — [[Source: Deep Dive into India's Consumption Story This Festive Season]].

The 5-10 year view: D2C-only $1B brands will emerge in India, but the current incentive structure (VC metrics require market share → requires marketplace presence) works against it. The US model also faces pressure from Shein, Temu, and TikTok Shop which are eroding brand.com's dominance.

## China-India Consumer IPO Parallel

China's consumer IPO trajectory provides a roadmap for India:
- China 1990-2000: negligible consumer IPOs (tail phase)
- China 2000-2010: GDP per capita 2.5x-ed → consumer index 5x → ~400-450 consumer IPOs (~40/year)
- China 2010-2015: consumer index another 5x → ~4,000 consumer IPOs (800-900/year)
- India's GDP per capita now ~$2,500, equivalent to China ~2002-2003
- Projection: at least 2,000 consumer IPOs in India over next 15 years
- Fashion alone is $125B with only $25B organized — massive runway

— [[Source: "The Indian Consumer at 2030" Report by Fireside Ventures]]

## Depth over Breadth as Winning Strategy

The old belief that "you can't build a big business staying in one niche" is now false. Niche depth at 5M customers can build ₹500 Cr+ businesses profitably. See [[Depth_over_Breadth|Depth over Breadth]] for the full framework — [[Source: "The Indian Consumer at 2030" Report by Fireside Ventures]].

## GT Margin Re-Rating Risk

Traditional FMCG ran ~25% EBIT margins due to General Trade (GT) dominance. In US/UK/Canada with organized trade, brands do 12-15% EBIT max. As India shifts to modern trade/e-commerce/quick commerce, structural EBIT compression is plausible but won't happen within 5 years — India won't go beyond ~35% organized trade due to political structure protecting the trader community — [[Source: "The Indian Consumer at 2030" Report by Fireside Ventures]].
