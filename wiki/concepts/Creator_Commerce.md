# Creator Commerce

## Definition

A commerce model where content creators serve as the primary discovery and conversion layer between sellers and buyers, replacing traditional search-based product discovery. Distinct from influencer marketing (one-off sponsored posts) — in creator commerce, the creator is a structural participant in the transaction flywheel, earning commissions on conversions.

## Meesho as the Indian Case Study

[[Meesho]]'s 4-sided marketplace is the clearest Indian example. Key mechanics:
- ~40,000 creators produce content like "10 saree designs under ₹149"
- The content drives both engagement (for the creator's audience) and conversion (impulse purchases)
- Creators earn commissions, incentivizing them to produce more content as merchant selection grows
- ₹2,000-3,000 Cr of NMV (~5% of ~₹20,000 Cr total) flows through this creator channel
- This follows the **China/SE Asia model** (Shein, Temu, TikTok Shop) rather than the **US model** (Amazon search-based discovery)

See [[Source: Meesho's Bharat Playbook — India's Only 4-Sided Marketplace]] for full analysis.

## India vs US vs China

| Dimension | US (Amazon) | China (TikTok Shop, Pinduoduo) | India (Meesho) |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------------------|----------------|
| Discovery | Search-based | Creator/social feed | Creator-affiliate |
| Affiliate marketing | Brand-funded, mature | Platform-integrated | Emerging |
| Brand.com viability | Strong ($1B+ D2C brands) | Weak (T-mall/JD dominate) | Small but growing |

In the US, [[Amazon India]] has not invested in the creator ecosystem the way US Amazon has with affiliate programs. In India, brands can spend on affiliate marketing on US Amazon but not Indian Amazon — [[Source: Meesho's Bharat Playbook — India's Only 4-Sided Marketplace]].

## Scale and 80-20 Rule

[[Nykaa]] works with ~50,000 creators, but only 500 drive ~80% of GMV. The same 80-20 will apply to Meesho — likely ~10,000 creators max will drive meaningful traffic, not all 40,000. Still a formidable distribution moat — [[Source: Meesho's Bharat Playbook — India's Only 4-Sided Marketplace]].

## Why Marketplaces Beat D2C for Creator Commerce

Creator commerce converts better on marketplaces than D2C because selection drives engagement. A creator posting "lipsticks under ₹99 this Diwali" links to a marketplace category page with dozens of options — conversion is optimized by selection. On D2C, the selection is limited by design, and conversion only spikes during deep-discount events (BOGO weeks). Affiliates are paid on conversion, not awareness — so they gravitate to platforms where conversion is highest — [[Source: Meesho's Bharat Playbook — India's Only 4-Sided Marketplace]].

## Evolution Potential

The ad model in creator commerce may evolve to brands funding creators directly (not PPC-style ads), essentially feeding the flywheel by encouraging creators and sellers to promote branded products. The China/TikTok model where the transaction closes within the entertainment app (no app-switch) is the aspiration — [[Source: Meesho's Bharat Playbook — India's Only 4-Sided Marketplace]].
