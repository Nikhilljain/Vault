# India SaaS Market

## Definition

The growing ecosystem of software-as-a-service companies building and selling in India, encompassing both the domestic market for software purchases and the Indian SaaS startup landscape. Historically dismissed as too small ("nobody pays for software in India"), the market is now reaching critical mass.

## Market Size and Trajectory

- 2020: ~$18B total Indian software and cloud market
- 2025: ~$35-36B
- Projected ~2032: $100B
- This is **recurring revenue** — AWS/GCP/Azure bills are modern utilities that can't be skipped
- Indian startups spend ~5x the software intensity of traditional companies — [[Source: Breaking Down India's SaaS Market — US Dollars vs Indian Distribution]]

## Why India-First SaaS Is Now Viable

### Domestic TAM Has Exploded
Consumer internet companies now generate massive software budgets. A single company like [[Swiggy]] represents $2M+ ACV. Across Swiggy, [[Zepto]], Eternal, [[Meesho]] etc., there's $25M+ of locked-in ACV just from consumer tech. [[Jio]] alone spends triple-digit millions on software annually — [[Source: Breaking Down India's SaaS Market — US Dollars vs Indian Distribution]].

### The D2C → SaaS Second-Order Effect
As [[D2C_Brand_Landscape_India|D2C brands]] eliminate traditional distribution middlemen, ~15% of the consumption shift flows into software purchases. Every D2C brand doing ₹100 Cr+ monthly needs marketing automation, logistics software, analytics — all SaaS — [[Source: Breaking Down India's SaaS Market — US Dollars vs Indian Distribution]].

### Enterprise Buy vs Build Shift
Indian CTOs now have 20+ vendor options in every software category. The CFO becomes the catalyst — Michael Porter's competition theory: when competitive vendor markets exist, buying always beats building internally. Conway's Law reinforces this: companies ship their org chart's complexity, so outsourcing non-core functions keeps the company simple. Exception: "the one thing that makes you special" — [[Source: Breaking Down India's SaaS Market — US Dollars vs Indian Distribution]].

### Five-City Sales Cluster
MMR, NCR, Bangalore, Singapore, Dubai — these five cities can take an Indian SaaS company to $25M ARR without touching the US. Sales cost in India: ₹30-40L base per rep with 6-7x ROI. US: $200K base with 2-3x ROI — [[Source: Breaking Down India's SaaS Market — US Dollars vs Indian Distribution]].

## The Broken "Go West" Playbook

VCs historically pushed Indian SaaS founders to sell in the US. Problems:
1. **Cultural barriers ("Brown Man Tax"):** Indian founders hire expensive US sales teams they can't culturally manage. No Monday Night Football conversations, no local networks. Unless the founder has US roots (e.g., [[Druva]] founder from CMU), network effects don't work.
2. **Cost structure trap:** [[Freshworks]] at $700-800M revenue still spends 50-60% of opex on sales & marketing, 24-48 month CAC payback, ~20% SBC. Compliance cost of US listing alone is ~1% of revenue.
3. **0-to-1 is harder in US:** In India, "five calls" can get you to $1M ARR through friends and family. In the US, it's a cold market with entrenched competition.
4. **Labor arbitrage disappeared:** Post-COVID remote work equalized salaries. Companies like Rippling pay 1-1.5 Cr packages in India — [[Source: Breaking Down India's SaaS Market — US Dollars vs Indian Distribution]].

## Forward Deployed Engineers (FDEs)

A new model replacing traditional Customer Success, originating from Palantir:
- Engineers go to customer offices and ship directly, rather than CSMs relaying feedback to product
- Reduces time-to-value dramatically
- Smaller customers get outsized service → grow faster → become bigger accounts
- Currently viable only for high-ACV clients
- Engineers are willing because coding alone isn't a defensible long-term skill
- Essentially "consulting mixed with engineering" — [[Source: Breaking Down India's SaaS Market — US Dollars vs Indian Distribution]]

## SaaS IPO Horizon

The first cohort of Indian SaaS IPOs is expected in 1-2 years:
- [[MoEngage]] raised $100M, possibly flipping to India for IPO
- [[CleverTap]] publicly discussing IPO plans
- [[Traction]] proved Indian markets can value SaaS (listed at $10M revenue)
- Expected eventually: Indian SaaS index ETF
- Companies of the 2014-2016 vintage are reaching the 10-year mark → IPO readiness — [[Source: Breaking Down India's SaaS Market — US Dollars vs Indian Distribution]]
