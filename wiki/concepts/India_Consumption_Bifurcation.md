# India Consumption Bifurcation

## Definition

The idea that India's consumption story is fundamentally split: the top 5% of the population (~70 million people, ~17 million households) is experiencing rapid, US-style consumption growth, while the bottom 95% sees stagnant or declining real consumption. Standard macroeconomic indicators (FMCG growth of legacy companies, core inflation) fail to capture this because they measure baskets skewed toward the broader population.

## The Two Indias

### India 1: Top 5% (~70M people)
- GDP per capita equivalent to Mexico (~$10-15K), possibly higher now
- Driving premiumization: Mercedes-Benz sold 25,000 cars in India (one every 6 minutes, ~₹25,000 Cr revenue)
- Benefiting from wealth effects: stock market access up from <2% to 6-7% of population in 5 years, plus real estate and gold appreciation
- Exhibiting US-style behavior: low savings, high credit-based consumption
- Savings rate at historic low of ~88.5% — [[Source: Deep Dive into India's Consumption Story This Festive Season]]

### India 2-4: The Rest
- Income/wealth increases are not meaningful beyond top 5%
- Legacy FMCG companies (Britannia, Unilever, etc.) report slowdowns — but this may reflect brand-switching to new-age D2C brands, not reduced consumption
- Insurance and credit penetration still lagging

## Why Standard Metrics Miss It

The ASYMMETRIC hosts argue that India's standard economic indices (Nifty, FMCG trackers) are biased toward legacy companies. ~160 new-age D2C brands collectively generate ₹42,000 Cr annually (growing 15-20%), now comparable to legacy FMCG companies ex-top 3 (₹45,000 Cr, growing 5%). But these new brands aren't in the index — leading analysts to incorrectly conclude that consumption is slowing — [[Source: Deep Dive into India's Consumption Story This Festive Season]].

## Geographic Comparison

An interesting comparison raised: India should benchmark against tropical, similar-GDP-per-capita countries (Mexico, not US or Korea) for consumer trend inspiration. The observation that no developed economy exists between the tropics above certain population thresholds adds nuance to India's growth trajectory — [[Source: Deep Dive into India's Consumption Story This Festive Season]].
