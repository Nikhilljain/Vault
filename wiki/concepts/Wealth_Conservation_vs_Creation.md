# Wealth Conservation vs Creation

## Definition

Two fundamentally different orientations for running a business. Wealth conservation (custodian mindset) focuses on protecting and growing existing value through dividends, low risk, and incremental growth. Wealth creation focuses on acquiring new capabilities, taking risks, and building value aggressively.

## Colgate vs Reckitt: The Canonical Examples

| Dimension | Colgate (Conservation) | Reckitt (Creation) |
|-----------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Age | Founded by William Colgate (died 150+ years ago) | Founded 1814, current form 26 years old |
| Revenue | ~$25B (incl. $10B pet foods/Hill's) | — |
| Market Cap | ~$140B | — |
| Power center | CFO (most powerful after CEO) | Revenue/profitability incentives |
| Approach | Protect core, high dividends | Acquisitions (Plex, Beardo), new categories |
| Risk tolerance | Low — custodian mindset | Higher — incentivized for growth |
| Key structure | 50-year JV in China (Darlie: ~$500M revenue, $250M profit to NY), 70-30 Pakistan partnership | Multiple acquisitions to stay relevant in digital brand creation |

Family offices worth ₹10,000 Cr keeping 90% in triple-A bonds at 9% is rational wealth conservation — [[Source: Why We're Pausing & Year-End Reflections]].

## Founder Mindset vs CEO Mindset

- **Founder:** Emotional, purpose-driven, willing to make non-economic decisions
- **CEO:** Maximize shareholder value, professional management
- **Tata example:** Air India acquisition was an "emotional purchase" that didn't make shareholder sense. Post Ratan Tata, family-driven emotional decisions become harder
- As founder shareholding dilutes and professional CEOs take over, orientation shifts toward conservation

## Post-Money Hierarchy: Money → Respect → Relevance

After accumulating wealth, people seek respect. After respect, they seek relevance. Tech billionaires in their 40s need to stay relevant for 30 more years — explains seemingly irrational behavior like bending the knee to political figures — [[Source: Why We're Pausing & Year-End Reflections]].

## China: Government-Enforced Conscious Capitalism

- Stock market returned 0% over 15-17 years despite 300%+ GDP growth
- Government capped ROE at 5-7%
- Only country independent from US money supply
- Operating collective > individual model — a government-enforced version of conscious capitalism

## IPO as Milestone vs Destination

Current IPO frenzy driven by aligned incentives (VCs, fund managers, content creators, retail). But some companies thrive staying private:
- **SpaceX:** $800B valuation, 60% financial investors, gives liquidity from profits without listing
- **Cargill:** $180B revenue, 87% family-owned, 100+ family members, fully private
