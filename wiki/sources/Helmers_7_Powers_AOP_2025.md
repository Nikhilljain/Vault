# Source: Helmer's 7 Powers — Choosing Your Moat Vector & What Your AOP Must Optimize For

**Type:** YouTube Video | **Channel:** [[Channel - ASYMMETRIC Podcast]]
**Date:** 2025-12-12 | **Duration:** ~1 hour | **Episode:** 59 (Guest-free)

## Key Takeaways

- **Moats are NOT permanent.** Biggest gap in how people think about Helmer's 7 Powers. For non-winner-take-all businesses (consumer brands), moats change over time. Scale economies might not matter at ₹100 Cr but become a moat at ₹5,000 Cr. Every D2C brand starts with counter-positioning as initial moat, but this erodes as competitors copy.

- **IndiGo scores ~8/10 on ALL seven powers.** Two-thirds market share in a duopoly. Started with ₹100 Cr of "street funding." Were "brutal in finishing off competition" (Kingfisher, Jet Airways, GoAir, SpiceJet all dead). Brand in aviation is "damn hard" — they did it even on sandwich wrappers. 3-day operational crisis was amplified by social media algorithms but recovered completely.

- **AOP (Annual Operating Plan) framework — What/How/When:** Becomes meaningful only after ~₹100-120 Cr revenue. BSC targets 60-70% growth (₹500 → ₹850 Cr). "What" should have 100% accuracy; "How" is the input that needs clarity before outputs can be fixed. Capital should be allocated toward moat-strengthening vectors, not just revenue growth.

- **Amazon Now is the "joker in the pack" in quick commerce:** Opening 2 dark stores every 2 days. Insane recommendation engine with pin-code level data on every product ordered. Network effects from day one — already knows what to stock per pin code. Multiple hosts personally switched and can't go back.

- **Google Gemini's ecosystem threat:** New unique search queries jumped from <5% to 15% due to AI prompting behavior. Gemini embedded across Chrome, Gmail, Maps, YouTube. 2.5-3B Android users = massive free-tier distribution. Potential existential threat to ChatGPT/Perplexity for mass consumer.

- **Lenskart's moat is scale economies + process power, NOT brand.** Delivers made-to-order spectacles in 48 hours under ₹2,000. New manufacturing plant every 2 years, unit cost declining. But Lenskart is a "retail destination," not a product brand — you wouldn't pay premium for the name like Ray-Ban.

- **Mamaearth has 9 days of working capital** — described as "insane" for their SKU complexity. Demonstrates process power as an under-appreciated moat.
