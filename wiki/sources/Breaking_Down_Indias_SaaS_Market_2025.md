# Source: Breaking Down India's SaaS Market — US Dollars vs Indian Distribution

**Type:** YouTube Video | **Channel:** [[Channel - ASYMMETRIC Podcast]]
**Date:** 2025-11-07 | **Duration:** 1:16:43 | **Episode:** 54
**Guest:** Kuldeep Dankar (co-founder LastNine, ex-CleverTap sales/CS head, ex-Nokia/Microsoft)

## Key Takeaways

- **India's software market is tripling:** Total Indian software and cloud market was ~$18B in 2020, currently ~$35-36B, expected to reach $100B in 7-8 years. This is all recurring revenue — modern utilities like AWS/GCP bills that can't be skipped. Indian startups spend ~5x the software intensity of traditional companies because software is their competitive edge.

- **Consumer internet creates massive SaaS TAM:** A single company like Swiggy now represents a $2M+ annual contract. Add Zepto, Eternal, Meesho — you quickly get $25M+ of locked-in ACV just from consumer internet companies. Jio alone spends triple-digit millions on software annually. The merger of buyer and consumer through D2C eliminates traditional distribution middlemen, and ~15% of this consumption shift flows into software.

- **The "Go West" playbook is broken:** VCs pushed Indian SaaS founders to sell in the US, but most lacked networks, cultural context, and operational experience there. [[Freshworks]] exemplifies the cost: 50-60% of operating expenses on sales & marketing, CAC payback of 24-48 months, ~20% share-based compensation. In India, a sales rep costs ₹30-40L (vs $200K in US) and delivers 6-7x ROI per rupee spent vs 2-3x in the US. The "brown man tax" — Indian founders hiring expensive US talent they can't culturally manage — compounds the problem.

- **India-first SaaS is now viable:** Companies can reach $25M ARR selling only in India across 5 cluster cities (MMR, NCR, Bangalore, Singapore, Dubai). Reaching $10M in India first gives you a battle-tested product, proven value prop, and the maturity to then expand to the US. Indian companies like Swiggy/Zomato operate at global-scale reliability — if your software serves them, you're already US-ready.

- **Buy vs Build is shifting decisively to Buy:** Indian CTOs now have 20+ vendor options in every software category. CFOs are the biggest catalyst — Michael Porter's competition theory means competitive vendor markets always beat internal teams. Conway's Law applies: companies ship the complexity of their org chart, so outsourcing non-core functions keeps the company simple. The only exception is when something is "the one thing that makes you special."

- **Forward Deployed Engineers (FDEs) replacing Customer Success:** Term from Palantir. Instead of account managers who relay feedback to product teams, companies now send engineers to sit in customer offices and ship directly. Reduces time-to-value, delights smaller customers who get outsized service. Currently viable only for high-ACV clients. Engineers are willing because they know coding alone isn't a defensible long-term skill.

- **SaaS IPO wave imminent:** [[MoEngage]] raised $100M (possibly flipping to India for IPO). [[CleverTap]] talking about going public. [[Traction]] proved Indian markets can value SaaS by listing at $10M revenue. Expectation: first cohort of Indian SaaS IPOs in 1-2 years, eventually leading to a SaaS index ETF.
