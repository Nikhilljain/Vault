# Source: Deep Dive into India's Consumption Story This Festive Season

**Type:** YouTube Video | **Channel:** [[Channel - ASYMMETRIC Podcast]]
**Date:** 2025-10-17 | **Duration:** 55:30 | **Episode:** 51

## Key Takeaways

- **India's consumption is bifurcated:** The top 5% of India (~70M people, GDP per capita ~$10-15K) is driving consumption growth with US-style spending behavior (low savings, credit-driven). The next 15 Cr people are seeing a slowdown. Mercedes-Benz sold one car every 6 minutes in India, hitting 25,000 cars and ~₹25,000 Cr revenue — but this reflects only the top tier.

- **Festive e-commerce exploded:** ₹60,000 Cr of online sales in the first 10 days of the 2025 festive season — roughly 5x growth YoY in the first two days alone. Macro tailwinds: repo rate cuts, tax-free limits increased, GST 2.0 rollout.

- **D2C brands are now as big as legacy FMCG:** ~160 new-age D2C brands (all founded <10 years ago) collectively generate ₹42,000 Cr annually, growing at 15-20% — comparable in scale to legacy companies like HUL, Britannia (₹45,000 Cr collectively ex-top 3), which grow at only ~5%. The standard indices don't capture this shift because they're weighted to old-economy stocks.

- **Credit-driven consumption is the new normal:** India's savings rate has fallen to a historic low of ~88.5%. Credit card and e-commerce transactions grew 27% by volume during the festive season. UPI growth is slowing (50% → 20% YoY) while credit card growth is replacing it. Credit on UPI is an emerging tailwind.

- **Auto and durables confirm the split:** Navratri 2025 saw 1.1M vehicles sold (up 36% YoY). Maruti delivered 30,000, Hyundai billed 11,000. Top 6 selling cars: only 1 sedan, rest SUV/MUV — Kia/Synet now #1, replacing Alto/Swift. Samsung premium TV sales jumped 2x. Refrigerator/washing machine/AC up 30%. LG's IPO thesis: AC household penetration is where growth is for next 3-5 years. [[Urban Company]] and [[Atomberg]] entering AC compressor space.

- **Hiring market is broken for 15+ year experience:** Massive surplus of senior talent (15+ year, top schools) struggling to find roles. Not that companies are shying away — the jobs don't exist. The "beginner's mindset" / unlearning muscle is critical. Entrepreneurship exercises this muscle even if the venture fails. But India lacks examples of failed entrepreneurs who came back to stellar corporate careers (unlike in VC). SD runs "fellowship" and "maverick" programs — institutional grooming bottom-up, McKinsey/P&G model.

- **AI as depth tool — with caveats:** Young people use breadth (life experiences) for instinct and AI for depth. Example: Yash (MIT stats professor) using ChatGPT for biostat code but guiding it with domain expertise. The hosts' critique: ChatGPT is designed for engagement and retention (like TikTok/Instagram), not for giving you hard truths. AI's incentive is to keep you addicted, not to help you learn through painful feedback.
