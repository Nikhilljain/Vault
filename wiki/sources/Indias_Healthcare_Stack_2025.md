# Source: Inside India's Healthcare Stack — Capex-Heavy Hospitals, Supply Gaps & Venture-Scale Hospital Alternatives

**Type:** YouTube Video | **Channel:** [[Channel - ASYMMETRIC Podcast]]
**Date:** 2025-12-05 | **Duration:** ~1 hour | **Episode:** 58
**Guest:** Varun Dubey (Founder, Super Health; ex-Practo, ex-Apollo, ex-Ola)

## Key Takeaways

- **India has a 3 million bed deficit:** Needs ~4M beds, has ~1M. Largest chain (Apollo/Manipal) has only ~10,000 beds. Total hospital industry is ~$100B, corporate hospitals only ~10% of it. India needs ~$700B in capex to fill the gap.

- **Hospital capex is 66% wasted on land + construction:** Real example: Rs 800 Cr land + Rs 250 Cr construction + Rs 250 Cr equipment + litigation = Rs 1,700-1,800 Cr for 800-900 beds (~Rs 2 Cr/bed). Land and construction have zero bearing on care quality. Super Health's cost per bed is 65-70% lower.

- **Super Health delivers same quality at half the price:** Bilateral knee replacement: ~Rs 5L (vs Rs 8-10L). Appendix: ~Rs 85K-1L (vs Rs 1.7-2L). Built hospital in 4 months. 50 beds, ~200 employees, 3.5-4 people per bed (vs Apollo's 7 per bed excluding doctors). No paper, no printers, no registration/billing/insurance departments.

- **Healthcare funnel metrics create perverse incentives:** OPD-to-IPD conversion tracked like SaaS metrics (industry average 8-12%, surgical specialties 15-20%). Hospital management pressures doctors below group average, treating patient care like a sales funnel.

- **Insurance economics are structurally broken:** IRDAI data shows claim ratios of 90-110%. Insurance needs ~50% margin to be viable, but best hospital (Max) does only 30% EBITDA. Driving full-stack integration: PB Fintech (PolicyBazaar) investing $200M+ to build hospitals; Narayana Health launching insurance.

- **Friction, not cost, kills healthcare compliance:** Diabetes medication adherence drops after 92 days on average (lifelong medication). Practo experiment: auto-shipping medicine to 2,000 patients → 80% compliance after 6 months. Even billionaires face the same wait-time friction. Fear of diagnosis is a "$100B idea" if solved.

- **Super Health's tech stack:** Entire operations on Slack (per-patient channels). Built "Super OA" — AI that listens to doctor-patient conversations and writes prescriptions. Plans to open-source all AI systems. Equipment from United Imaging (Chinese manufacturer, found "vastly superior" and provided API-level access).
