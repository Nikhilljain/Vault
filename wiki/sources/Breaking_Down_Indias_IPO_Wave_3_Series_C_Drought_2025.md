# Source: Breaking Down India's IPO Wave 3, The Series C Drought

**Type:** YouTube Video | **Channel:** [[Channel - ASYMMETRIC Podcast]]
**Date:** 2025-10-24 | **Duration:** 46:25 | **Episode:** 52

## Key Takeaways

- **IPO Wave 3 is here and looks more solid than 2021:** 2025 has already seen 16 startup IPOs, nearing the 2021 highs. 7 companies listed, 14 more have filed. The pipeline is ~3.5x the current listed count. Key difference from Wave 1: more companies have a clear path to profitability this time.

- **Big 5 upcoming IPOs:** [[Lenskart]] (November, near certain), [[Groww]], [[Meesho]] (SEBI approval received, ~$6-7B valuation expected), [[boAt]], and [[Razorpay]]. Most expected to list before March 2026 (FY26 end). The IPO window has been open for 18-24 months, which is historically rare.

- **Series C funding has collapsed — crossover funds disappeared:** Series C capital deployed in India in 2025 (~$5B) is at 2017 levels. Root cause: global crossover funds that dominated Series C+ (2012-2024) have withdrawn. 8 Roads (Fidelity) merged into Asia-Pacific team. Multiple billion-dollar funds quietly stopped investing. This pushes companies into premature IPOs.

- **DPI is finally happening for Indian VC:** After years of complaints about lack of liquidity, distributions are materializing. Peak Capital expected to have $4-5B in public positions across portfolio. However, liquidity still comes in year 12-14 of fund life; the goal is to bring it to year 8-10. Companies born 2015-2020 are exiting faster than the 2008-2015 cohort.

- **Secondary market is dysfunctional:** Secondary funds need minimum ₹50-100 Cr tickets. But sellers won't sell 18-24 months before an IPO — private markets value on 6-month forward (FY26), public markets on 18-24 month forward (FY27-28). Waiting pays. This means private liquidity is frozen for the most promising companies. SEBI timeline reduced from 12-18 months to 3-4 months, making the IPO path even more attractive vs secondary.

- **TRMG startup index outperforming Nifty and S&P 500:** But 2/3 of startups that went public are NOT yet profitable. 90% of listed value is still traditional companies. The argument is being made that the index itself needs to be rebased to reflect the new-age economy.

- **Fund return expectations:** Indian venture funds need to deliver 5x in 10 years as the minimum norm (not 3x). Without 25%+ base-case returns, domestic LPs won't commit risk capital for 10-year illiquid holdings. Larger fund sizes depend on this benchmark becoming standard.
