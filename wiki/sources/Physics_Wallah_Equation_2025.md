# Source: Physics Wallah Equation — Low ARPU + Low CAC + High NPS = ₹3,000 Cr Revenue

**Type:** YouTube Video | **Channel:** [[Channel - ASYMMETRIC Podcast]]
**Date:** 2025-11-21 | **Duration:** 40:29 | **Episode:** 56 (Light Episode)
**Special Credit:** Harnoon Saini (prep on PW IPO analysis)

## Key Takeaways

- **PW is the first VC-backed edtech to IPO in India.** Revenue ~₹2,800-3,000 Cr total, ₹1,400 Cr from online (estimated ~90% gross margin). Company registered in 2020 — less than 5 years old at IPO. Created ~$3B+ in value. Marketing spend is only 10% of revenue vs 40-50%+ for competitors.

- **The PW Equation: Low ARPU + Low CAC + High NPS.** Annual courses at ₹3,000-3,600 per student (vs competitors at ₹40,000+). Zero paid acquisition — earned media via YouTube (~100M subscribers across group channels). High NPS because PW doesn't sell outcomes ("your child will get into Google"), so expectations stay realistic. This creates natural defensibility: nobody else wants to compete at this low-ARPU price point.

- **Content-to-Commerce is PW's real model.** [[Alakh_Pandey|Alakh Pandey]] started the YouTube channel in 2016, scaled for 4-5 years on YouTube ads. Launched the PW app in 2020. Unlike MrBeast/Logan Paul (creator sells separate product), Alakh IS the product. The creator flywheel and business flywheel run separately but the business latches onto earned media from the creator content.

- **Offline expansion to 500 centers.** Currently 300+ centers, funded by IPO proceeds. Centers need 12+ months to mature for healthy EBITDA — current losses are largely a function of expansion. PW chose to build rather than acquire (citing BYJU's-Aakash failed integration). PMF cannot be bought.

- **IPO was under-subscribed — hosts consider it a market miss.** Attributed to: social media noise, co-founder resignations before IPO, two years of margin degrowth from offline expansion, and struggles in non-core categories. At the valuation, hosts believe it should not have been under-subscribed.

- **AI disruption risk is real.** Litmus AI: while watching a YouTube physics video, you can pause and an AI teacher avatar explains concepts in your language. Could make education content free. PW is well-positioned with foundational datasets but must stay ahead.

- **"Three Roles" framework for founders:** Founder = emotional state of mind (should be dropped once company matures), Shareholder = economic interest, CEO = operational responsibility. PW's professional CEO gave the IPO speech, not Alakh — business is being de-risked from the creator.
