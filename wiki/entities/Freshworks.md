# Freshworks

**Type:** SaaS Company (US-listed) | **Category:** CRM, Customer Support, IT Service Management
**Revenue:** ~$700-800M (as of late 2025)

## Overview

Freshworks is an Indian-founded SaaS company that entered the CRM/helpdesk category as a challenger (did not create the category). Listed on NASDAQ, it serves as a cautionary example of the costs of the "Go West" SaaS playbook for Indian companies.

## Financial Red Flags (US Market Cost)

- **Sales & Marketing:** 50-60% of operating expenses — far above healthy SaaS benchmarks
- **CAC Payback:** Was 48 months, improved to ~24 months — still poor (worse than a McDonald's store payback)
- **Share-Based Compensation:** ~18-20% of operating expenses
- **US Compliance Cost:** ~1% of revenue just for listing compliance (shareholder relations office, forward-looking statement restrictions, etc.)
- Growth has flattened as churn accumulates — "SaaS seven-year itch" where customers start exploring competitors after 3-4 years

All data from [[Source: Breaking Down India's SaaS Market — US Dollars vs Indian Distribution]].

## The Structural Problem

Freshworks exemplifies the cost trap of Indian companies selling in the US without deep local roots. US sales reps cost $150-200K base + commissions, yielding only 2-3x ROI vs 6-7x in India. The company chased US market share "at whatever cost," and the stock reflects this concern — will the company ever achieve sustainable profitability? — [[Source: Breaking Down India's SaaS Market — US Dollars vs Indian Distribution]]
